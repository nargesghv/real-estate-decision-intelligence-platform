# KPI Framework

KPIs were selected from business decisions, not only from data availability.

## Stakeholder Questions
- Which region should we invest in?
- Is demand increasing?
- Is supply becoming risky?
- Are prices growing sustainably?
- What happens if interest rates rise?
- How confident is the forecast?

## KPI Selection Rules
Each KPI had to be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

## Core KPIs
1. Housing Demand Growth
2. Price Growth
3. Employment Growth
4. Inventory / Housing Supply
5. Construction Permits
6. Affordability Index = Average Price / Median Income
7. Months of Supply = Inventory / Sales Volume
8. Rent Yield = Annual Rent / Price
9. Forecast Uncertainty = Upper Forecast - Lower Forecast
10. Investment Score = weighted combination of demand, growth, yield, affordability risk, and uncertainty

## How KPIs Were Evaluated
- Historical trend analysis
- Regional benchmarking
- Stakeholder validation
- Correlation with investment outcomes
- Dashboard adoption
- Backtesting against forecast results
